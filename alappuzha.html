Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a city located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is a popular tourist destination known for its backwaters, beaches, and unique culture. Here are some of the highlights of tourism in Alappuzha:

Backwaters:

Alappuzha is famous for its intricate network of backwaters, which are a series of interconnected canals, lagoons, and lakes that are used for transportation and fishing. The best way to explore the backwaters is by taking a leisurely boat ride or a houseboat tour. The backwaters are also home to a variety of bird species and offer a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Beaches:

Alappuzha is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in Kerala. The most popular beach is the Alappuzha Beach, which is known for its soft sand, clear water, and picturesque sunsets. Other beaches in the area include Marari Beach, Andhakaranazhi Beach, and Thumpoly Beach.

Ayurvedic spas:

Kerala is known for its ancient system of medicine known as Ayurveda, which uses natural herbs and oils to promote wellness and relaxation. Alappuzha has many Ayurvedic spas that offer a range of treatments and massages to help visitors unwind and rejuvenate.

Houseboat tours:

One of the unique experiences in Alappuzha is a houseboat tour, where visitors can stay onboard a traditional Kettuvallam boat and explore the backwaters at a leisurely pace. The houseboats are equipped with modern amenities and offer a chance to experience the local culture and cuisine.

Nehru Trophy Boat Race:

Alappuzha is famous for its annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, which takes place on the second Saturday of August every year. The race features traditional snake boats, which are long and narrow boats that can hold up to 100 rowers. The race is a vibrant celebration of local culture and attracts visitors from all over the world.

Overall, Alappuzha is a beautiful and unique destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the rich culture and natural beauty of Kerala.
